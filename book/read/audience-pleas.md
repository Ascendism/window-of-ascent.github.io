# A note to readers — two doors, one book

This is one argument, not two books wearing different costumes. If you came in through different doors, you may need different permission slips before the same chapters can do their work.

## If you lead with land, water, and living systems

You may already be tired of being told that your skepticism is backward. You have seen extraction dressed as progress. You have watched tools arrive on terms set by people who do not live with the consequences on the ground.

**This book is not asking you to stop noticing that.** It is asking you to aim the noticing at the right layer: not every hammer, but who owns the mill, who writes the contract, who benefits when the default is fear.

Stay for the chapters on incentives, enclosure, and infrastructure. The examples will sometimes sound like repos and roadmaps. That is deliberate. The same pattern that mis-aims panic at a model also mis-aims it at a composting rule without touching the incentive underneath. You do not have to become a different kind of person to use the argument. You have to refuse the flatter story.

## If you lead with systems you build and run

You may already be tired of being told that your curiosity is complicity. You track releases, vendors, and failure modes for a living. You know tools change faster than institutions.

**This book is not asking you to stop building.** It is asking you to build with the incentive layer in view: what the stack is optimized to extract, who pays when the default shifts, what still works when the API price doubles or the feature you depended on disappears.

Stay for the chapters on soil, tenure, and material limits. The examples will sometimes sound like watersheds and county lines. That is not lifestyle cosplay. It is the floor your systems actually run on. Permaculture shows up here as design language—feedback loops, edges, yields—not as a brand. If that vocabulary is unfamiliar, treat it as another systems diagram. The discipline is the same one you already use when something fails in production.

## What we are not doing

We are not publishing two editions so you can pick the team that flatters you. We are not asking you to perform the other side's identity. We are asking you to read past your first reflex long enough to see structure.

If a sentence sounds like it was written for someone else, stay anyway. The next chapter may be where your door and theirs meet.
